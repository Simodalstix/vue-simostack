## Candidate formula

- Person harm: 50% — Person offences other than occupational assaults against police/emergency workers and dangerous/negligent acts such as dangerous driving.
- Residential burglary: 30% — Residential aggravated and non-aggravated burglary only; commercial and unknown-location burglary are excluded.
- Vehicle crime: 15% — Motor vehicle theft and theft from a motor vehicle.
- Property damage: 5% — Criminal damage only; graffiti and other property damage categories are excluded.

Every basket is converted to a smoothed relative risk percentile. The weighted risk is inverted to a 0–10 candidate Safety score.

## Interpretation limits

- The source measures offences recorded by Victoria Police, not all crime or personal experience of safety.
- Suburb counts have no publisher-supplied suburb population rate.
- Activity centres, hospitals, nightlife and transport hubs can attract offences committed by or against non-residents.
- CSA replaces sensitive A10 and A30 counts of three or fewer with two when calculating totals.
- Recorded data is extracted 18 days after the reference period and can move in later releases.
